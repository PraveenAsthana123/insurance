# NPS Interpretation & Detractor Playbook

## Score definitions

- **Promoters (9–10)** — likely to recommend, lowest churn.
- **Passives (7–8)** — satisfied but easy to poach.
- **Detractors (0–6)** — at-risk AND likely to damage word-of-mouth.
- **NPS = %Promoters − %Detractors**. Range: -100 to +100.

## Industry benchmarks (BEV / retail / telco)

- **Grocery / BEV brands**: NPS 30–45 is typical; above 55 is excellent.
- **Telco / subscription**: NPS 0–20 is typical; above 35 is excellent.
- **Luxury / specialty retail**: NPS 60–75 is expected.
- **Banking**: NPS 0–30 typical (customers rarely "love" a bank).

Interpreting cross-industry comparison is misleading. Always compare to your own industry + channel.

## Detractor routing (mandatory within 24 hours)

1. **Triage by verbatim** — NLP-classify the free-text reason into: product, service, billing, delivery, other.
2. **Product / service detractors** — route to the product squad owner; SLA 3 business days for a substantive reply.
3. **Billing / delivery detractors** — route to support manager for immediate outreach; SLA 1 business day.
4. **Unspecified detractor (no verbatim)** — send a short survey with pre-built options; do NOT call yet.
5. **Log the detractor in the VoC corpus** — feeds the monthly insight review.

## Promoter leverage

Promoters are rarely over-utilized. Specific uses:

- **Referral programs** — promoters convert at 2–3× the rate of general customers. Ask explicitly, with a share-friendly creative.
- **Testimonials / case studies** — the best ones come from recent promoters (< 60 days since the score).
- **Product research** — promoters will often beta-test new features willingly. Use their goodwill to validate (not to validate ideas you already committed to).

## Survey timing that maximizes signal

- **Post-purchase**: 2–5 days after delivery for e-commerce; immediately after service call for telco.
- **Anniversary**: once per 12 months for relationship-NPS; do NOT exceed this cadence.
- **Interaction-based**: after a support call or major milestone (first-month, first-year) — use transactional NPS (tNPS) and tag the event.

## Common anti-patterns

- **Chasing the score, not the feedback** — teams that optimize only NPS often end up with a short-term score bump followed by a churn spike.
- **Gaming the survey** (pre-qualifying who gets it) — invalidates benchmarks and misleads leadership.
- **Too-frequent surveying** — NPS survey fatigue drops response rate below 10%; below that, the sample is severely biased toward customers with strong opinions (usually negative).
- **Treating the score as single-number truth** — always report NPS alongside response rate, top 5 detractor themes, and save-action count.

## From NPS to action

A typical cycle:

1. Weekly: detractor triage + routing (above).
2. Bi-weekly: theme analysis of detractor verbatims; top 3 themes become a candidate backlog item for product or ops.
3. Monthly: readout to leadership: NPS trend, top themes, closure rate on detractor actions.
4. Quarterly: validate that closed actions actually moved NPS in the affected cohort (measurement, not anecdote).
